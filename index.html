  1. In 2014

    I created a "Hello, World!" program for the first time.

    My journey into programming began with the book ‘Computer Programming’ by Tamim Shahriar Subeen. I was in class 7 when I picked it up from the book fair, not realizing it would spark a lifelong interest in coding.

    The book introduced me to the fundamentals of the C programming language, and as I practiced, I began solving mathematical problems through code — a truly eye-opening experience

  2. In 2015

    Visual Studio Code & Fiverr Account

    In 2015, I started creating Windows applications using Visual Studio — mostly just for fun. I made some basic software that didn’t really have much practical use, but I truly enjoyed the process of building them.

    Around that time, I was also active on some popular tech websites like Techtunes and Tunerpage, where I occasionally posted tech-related content.

    Later that year, I opened a Fiverr account purely out of interest. I wasn’t planning to start freelancing seriously then, so I simply created the account and left it inactive for a while.

  3. Back in 2017

    Started Collage Life

    My Achievements

    During my college years, academic pressure increased, and I gradually became less active in the tech field. At one point, I got deeply obsessed with solving Rubik’s Cubes — I could solve a 3×3 cube in around 22 seconds after a lot of practice. Even now, I sometimes pick up the cube just for fun, though I no longer practice timing like I used to.

    What I learned

    College life taught me how demanding and overwhelming things can get — but also how to keep moving forward even when motivation fades.

  4. 2019 — Finding My Direction

    Started Dealing Clients Online

    It was a year of transition — from college life to preparing for university. More importantly, it was when I discovered my passion for technology and made the choice to study Computer Science and Engineering.

  5. 2022 — Exploring New Horizons

    From textbooks to real projects

    By 2022, I was deeply immersed in my Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) studies. While academic pressure was high, I began exploring areas beyond the classroom — from programming projects to tech blogs and discussions.

    I realized that university was not just about grades but about building practical skills, understanding problem-solving at scale, and developing a mindset for innovation. This was the year I started experimenting with real-world applications and understanding the industry landscape.

  6. 2023 — Building Skills and Projects

    Building, breaking, learning, and improving every step of the way

    In 2023, I shifted my focus from theoretical learning to hands-on projects. I worked on multiple software applications, small-scale systems, and coding exercises that strengthened my problem-solving abilities. I also started contributing to online tech communities, sharing insights, and learning from peers. It was a year of refining technical skills, understanding modern tools, and experimenting with ideas that blended creativity and functionality. The process was not always easy, but the learning curve was steep and immensely rewarding.

  7. 2024 — Real-World Exposure and Freelancing

    Turning knowledge into action

    By 2024, I began connecting my skills to real-world scenarios. I started freelancing on platforms like Fiverr and building projects that had practical applications. I also focused on web development, database management, and small software solutions, learning to manage clients, timelines, and expectations. While I was still a student, these experiences gave me a sense of professional responsibility and a taste of independence. I learned how to combine technical expertise with communication, time management, and client interaction — essential skills for a tech career.

  8. 2025 — Consolidating Skills and Vision

    2025 is about clarity and direction

    In 2025, I focused on consolidating my knowledge and aligning it with long-term goals. I worked on advanced projects, experimented with new technologies, and started planning a portfolio of work that reflects my capabilities. I also revisited my early tech passions — programming, problem-solving, and innovative thinking — to ensure they guide my career decisions. This year has been about combining experience, skill, and vision into a clear professional trajectory, preparing for the next chapter after university.
